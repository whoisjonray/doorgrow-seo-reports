Technical SEO

❌
Domain Identity Crisis Critical
The business operates on three different domains: propspecific.com redirects to propertyspecificrealty.com (the realty site), while propertyspecificmanagement.com is an entirely separate property management site. The canonical URL points to propertyspecificrealty.com, but users may be typing or searching for propspecific.com. This splits brand authority and confuses search engines about which domain to rank.
❌
Sitemap References Wrong Domain Critical
The robots.txt on propspecific.com references a sitemap at https://propertyspecificrealty.com/sitemap_index.xml. This is technically correct since propspecific.com redirects, but it means Google may not properly crawl all pages. The sitemap itself references propertyspecificrealty.com URLs only.
❌
Null Schema Markup Output Critical
The homepage contains a <script type="application/ld+json">null</script> tag, which is invalid structured data. This could trigger errors in Google Search Console and tells Google something is broken on the page.
⚠️
Excessive Render-Blocking Resources Warning
The homepage loads 22 CSS files and 39 JavaScript files, with at least 4 render-blocking scripts loaded in the <head> without defer or async. jQuery and jQuery Migrate are loaded synchronously. The raw HTML alone is 157KB. This dramatically slows initial page load.
⚠️
Duplicate Google Analytics Tags Warning
Two separate Google Analytics properties are installed: G-3TH7B2S1B2 and G-K1QWTQ2XTE, plus a Google Tag Manager container (GTM-PWB8LDG9). Duplicate tracking inflates pageview counts, skews analytics data, and adds unnecessary load time.
✅
HTTPS Properly Configured Good
HTTP redirects to HTTPS correctly via 301 redirect. SSL certificate is valid and served through Cloudflare CDN.
✅
Yoast SEO Plugin Installed Good
Yoast SEO v24.9 is active and generating meta tags, canonical URLs, Open Graph data, and XML sitemaps. Good foundation, but several outputs need attention (see issues above).
✅
Mobile Viewport Set Good
The viewport meta tag is properly configured with width=device-width, initial-scale=1. A dedicated mobile header component is also present.
✅
Robots.txt Present and Functional Good
The robots.txt properly blocks admin areas and IDX search result pages (which could create duplicate content issues). It allows /wp-admin/admin-ajax.php and /wp-content/.

On-Page SEO

⚠️
Title Tag Doesn't Target Property Management Warning
Current title: Property Specific Realty | Full Service Real Estate Firm. Carl's business focuses on property management, but the title only mentions "Real Estate Firm." There's no mention of property management, Wake Forest, NC, or any location keywords. A better title would target the actual service and location.
⚠️
Meta Description is Generic Warning
Current meta description: "Serving The Raleigh, Wake Forest and Surrounding Areas | We are known for offering a masterful market education call for potential buyers and sellers." This reads more like an internal tagline than a search-optimized description. It doesn't mention property management, specific services, or include a call to action.
⚠️
H1 Tag is Vague Warning
The H1 tag reads: Full Service Real Estate Firm Serving the Raleigh, Wake Forest and Surrounding Areas. While it includes location, it's inside the hero slider and styled as a tagline. There's only 1 H1 tag (good), but it doesn't target property management keywords at all.
❌
Weak Heading Hierarchy Critical
The only H2 tags on the page are Main Content (a hidden accessibility tag) and CTA (a screen-reader-only tag). There are no visible, SEO-functional H2 or H3 headings. Section titles like "Featured Properties" are rendered inside <span> tags inside generic divs, not proper heading tags. This means Google can't understand the page structure.
⚠️
20 Images Missing Alt Text Warning
Of ~90 images on the page, 20 have no alt attribute at all. While most property listing images do have descriptive alt text (address-based), the missing ones represent lost opportunities for image search visibility and accessibility compliance.
⚠️
Logo Alt Text Says "Lori Webb" Not Company Name Warning
The site logo's alt text is Lori Webb instead of Property Specific Realty or Property Specific Management. This is repeated across at least 4 logo instances. The alt text for logos should match the business name for brand signal consistency.
✅
Canonical URL Set Good
A canonical tag is properly set to https://propertyspecificrealty.com/, which prevents duplicate content issues from the propspecific.com redirect.
✅
Open Graph Tags Present Good
OG title, description, image, and URL are all set. Twitter card is configured as summary_large_image. Social sharing should display correctly.

Local SEO

❌
Phone Number Mismatch in Schema vs. Display Critical
The RealEstateAgent schema markup lists the phone number as +1-919-0307-7767, but the phone number displayed on the website is 919.263.1291. This inconsistency confuses Google and can hurt local search rankings. NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency is one of the top local ranking factors.
⚠️
Schema Lists "Lori Webb" but Business is "Property Specific" Warning
The footer copyright says © 2026. Lori Webb. All rights reserved., logo alt text says "Lori Webb", and the schema lists the business separately as "Property Specific Realty." For Carl Driver's property management business, this creates brand confusion. Google may not know which entity to associate with the business.
⚠️
No LocalBusiness or PropertyManagement Schema Warning
While a RealEstateAgent schema exists (good), there's no LocalBusiness or property-management-specific schema. For property management searches, Google needs explicit signals about PM services, service areas, and business type.
✅
RealEstateAgent Schema Has Address Good
The structured data includes the business address: 143 Wait Ave, Wake Forest, NC 27587. It also includes social profile links (Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn) via sameAs. Good foundation for local signals.
✅
Google Verification Tags Present Good
Both Google Site Verification and Bing Webmaster verification tags are present, indicating the site is connected to both search console platforms.

Performance & Page Speed

❌
Massive Page Weight Critical
The raw HTML document alone is 157KB before any CSS, JS, images, or fonts load. Combined with 22 CSS files and 39 JS files, the total page weight is likely well over 3MB. Google recommends keeping total page size under 1.5MB for optimal performance. This directly impacts Core Web Vitals scores.
⚠️
Too Many Third-Party Requests Warning
The page makes requests to resources.agentimage.com, fonts.googleapis.com, cdn.jsdelivr.net, js.hs-scripts.com (HubSpot), www.googletagmanager.com, www.google.com/recaptcha, cdn.photos.sparkplatform.com (MLS photos), and more. Each domain requires a DNS lookup and TLS handshake, adding latency.
✅
Lazy Loading Implemented Good
The site uses both native loading="lazy" and a JavaScript lazy loader (Perfmatters + LazySizes). Images below the fold are deferred. The hero image uses loading="eager" with preload hints, which is correct.
✅
Cloudflare CDN Active Good
The site is served through Cloudflare (confirmed by cf-ray headers), which provides edge caching, DDoS protection, and performance optimization.
✅
Perfmatters Plugin Active Good
The Perfmatters performance plugin is installed and delaying non-critical scripts. It's also handling YouTube lazy loading and CSS background lazy loading. This is a premium optimization plugin being put to work.

The PM site (propertyspecificmanagement.com) appears to run on a different platform (NestHub/custom). Its OG URL references propspecific.com while its canonical is propertyspecificmanagement.com. Prioritized Action Plan

1
Fix Phone Number in Schema Markup ▲ HIGH IMPACT
Update the RealEstateAgent structured data phone number from +1-919-0307-7767 to match the actual displayed number 919.263.1291. NAP consistency is foundational for local SEO. This is a 5-minute fix with immediate impact.
2
Remove the Null Schema Tag ▲ HIGH IMPACT
Delete the <script type="application/ld+json">null</script> line from the homepage template. This is generating structured data errors in Search Console and can hurt crawling.
3
Consolidate Domain Strategy ▲ HIGH IMPACT
Decide on a single domain for all SEO authority. Options: (A) Make propertyspecificrealty.com the primary and add a /property-management/ section for PM content, or (B) build propspecific.com as the primary brand. Either way, stop splitting authority across 3 domains.
4
Add Proper Heading Structure ▲ HIGH IMPACT
Convert section titles (Featured Properties, About, Areas, etc.) from <span> tags to proper <h2> tags. Add <h3> tags for sub-sections. This gives Google a clear page outline and boosts topical relevance.
5
Rewrite Title Tag and Meta Description ▲ HIGH IMPACT
New title suggestion: Property Specific | Property Management & Real Estate in Wake Forest, NC. New meta: Include services (property management, buying, selling), location (Wake Forest, Raleigh, NC), and a call to action.
6
Add Homepage Content (300+ Words) ▲ MEDIUM IMPACT
Write unique, keyword-rich content for the homepage that describes property management services, areas served, and why clients choose Property Specific. The current 60 words isn't enough for Google to understand the page.
7
Remove Duplicate Google Analytics Tags ▲ MEDIUM IMPACT
Keep one GA4 property and manage it through GTM. Remove the duplicate gtag.js installation. This reduces page load and fixes data accuracy issues.
8
Fix Logo Alt Text and Copyright ▲ MEDIUM IMPACT
Change all logo alt attributes from "Lori Webb" to "Property Specific Realty" or "Property Specific Management." Update the footer copyright to reflect the current business ownership.
9
Add Missing Image Alt Text ▲ MEDIUM IMPACT
Add descriptive alt text to the 20 images currently missing it. Use descriptive, keyword-relevant text (not keyword stuffing). Prioritize images in the main content area.
10
Add Property Management Schema ▲ MEDIUM IMPACT
Add a LocalBusiness schema with additionalType for property management. Include services offered, service area, hours, and ensure NAP matches Google Business Profile exactly.
11
Optimize Page Speed ▲ LOWER IMPACT
Reduce CSS/JS file count by combining files, defer remaining render-blocking scripts, and optimize image delivery. The Agent Image theme loads a lot of resources. Consider whether all 22 CSS and 39 JS files are truly needed.
12
Upgrade PM Site Analytics ▲ LOWER IMPACT
The property management site still uses Universal Analytics (deprecated July 2023). Migrate to GA4 before all historical data is lost. Connect both sites' analytics for a unified view of the business.
